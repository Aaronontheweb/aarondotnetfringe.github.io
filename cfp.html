WHAT IS .NET FRINGE?

.NET Fringe is a community of developers working on the edges of technology, creating new and interesting open source projects. Our conferences create a time and a place for this community to come together, share, discuss, and work together to build the next generation of amazing .NET software.

We think the .NET platform is more interesting today than it ever has been. The war is over and the notion that Microsoft and Open Source are at odds with one-another is antiquated. ALT.NET, which was founded on the idea of that conflict, is no longer relevant. .NET Fringe is a new community based on trust and collaboration, instead of conflict.

Come be a part of this fantastic gathering of open-source .NET developers!

TOPIC IDEAS

The general focus of .NET Fringe is open-source .NET software. Within that thematic scope, we love having wide-ranging content that will stretch people in new directions. We have a strong interest in emerging technologies and new projects that might not yet be appreciated by the mainstream .NET world.

Some of our favorite talks are ones that make people see the world in a new way. Teaching entirely new ideas to others can be magical, and we are always delighted with the breadth of knowledge that our community brings together.

We love talks that deliver hard-earned knowledge based on real-world experience and research. Whether it's a story about scaling your backend servers by switching to F#, a new hardware project based on the Netduino or a sociological inquiry into the role of open source in a corporate driven world, come share it with people who are eager to know.

PRESENTATION FORMAT

Presentations will be 30 minutes long. If you have specific length needs for your presentation, let us know and we can work with it.

SPEAKER BENEFITS AND LOGISTICS

Speaking at .NET Fringe is a fantastic way to participate in the conference, and also comes with a few tangible benefits:

  • If you are selected to speak at .NET Fringe, we will waive your attendance fee, and also do our best to cover travel and expenses (we're still working out the details of what we'll be able to cover).
  • If you submit a proposal, but are not selected, we will offer you a heavily discounted ticket to show our appreciation. You'll also be first in line to give a lightning talk on the same topic.
